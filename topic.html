Introduction to our Workshop


One of the set goals of First-Aid is called "The three P's" and those are : Preserve life, Prevent further Injuries, and Promote recovery. We are promoting the Three P's to our workshop. First-Aid is an important tool in responding to accidents and ensures that injuries cand be efficiently and promptly dealth with.

First Aid


It is the first and immediate assistance given to any person suffering from either a minor or serious illness injury, with care provided to preserve life, prevent the illness or injury from worsening and to promote early recovery.


Lecture # Topic Details & Description
1 Core Values to Possess - Participants must be able to control themselves when they are going to perform simple medical procedures.

- Emotions must not interfere when doing first aids.

- At the end of the session, there will be a surprise simulation of an accident to determine whether the participants had taken any rightful measures or not.
2 Introduction to First-Aid Basics & Proper Precautionary Measures

- Simple medical procedures will be tackled to boost the interest of the participants.

- Introduction to medical terms.

- Simple wound dressing and simple rescue procedure will be tackled.

- At the end of the series lesson #2, participants will have a simulation on how to dress wounds properly and other simple medical procedures.
3 Medical Problems, Medical Equipments, and Human Anatomical Figure Lesson Proper

- A detailed series lesson wherein this will be the foundation of first aid workshop.

- Introduction of every kind of patient and different medical procedures according to the situation.

At the end of the series lesson #3, participants will collaborate on a worksheet to answer practical questions based on the previous topics.

3.1 Area Of Injury

- A lesson series where kinds and areas of injuries are conveyed.

- At the end of the series lesson #3.1, participants must know how to transport patients based on the condition.

- Participants must know how to at least do 5 different techniques in doing bandages according to the area of injury.

- Warm-up activity #1 will be done again.

3.2 Traumatic Injuries

- A lesson series where types of injuries are classified based on its severity

- At the end of the lesson series #3.2, participants are going to take a basic test determining the amount of knowledge they have gained from the past 2 lesson series.

3.3 Congenital Diseases

- A lesson series where different types of congenital disease are conveyed.

- At the end of the lesson series #3.3, participants will have an individual written and practical assessment based on lesson series 3.1 to 3.3.
4 First-Aid Response according to Illness or Injury

- A lesson series where it introduces and breaks down the broad processes of medical treatments according to injured patients or illness.

- At the end of the lesson series #4, participants will do a simulation of first aid response according to the given situation.
5 Introduction to CPR, Intubation, and Simple Operational Response Lesson Proper

- A lesson series that introduces resuscitation to near dead patients

- A lesson series that tackles different operational response with use of simple medical equipments or alternative to resuscitate

- At the end of the lesson series #5, resuscitation simulation course will be held

5.1 Emergency CPR, Intubation, and Simple Operational Response for Infant

- A lesson series that covers human anatomies of babies and risks when doing emergency responses

- Proper procedure of resuscitation to babies will be conveyed.

- At the end of the lesson series #5.1, there will be a collaborative practical assessment to create a prototype of an infant and demonstrate proper emergency response according to the type of situation
5.2 Emergency CPR, Intubation, and Simple Operational Response for a Child

- A lesson series that covers human anatomies of children and risks when doing emergency responses

- Proper procedure of resuscitation to children will be conveyed.

- At the end of the lesson series #5.2, practical assessment will be held to differentiate the processes needed to do between a child and an infant
5.3 Emergency CPR, Intubation, and Simple Operational Response for an Adult

- A lesson series that covers human anatomies of adults and risks when doing emergency responses

- Proper procedure of resuscitation to adults will be conveyed.

- At the end of the lesson series #5.3, real CPR course will be held.
6 Drowning

- A lesson series that covers factors that happens to human body when drowning

- Continuation of the lesson series #5 of medical responses

- At the end of the lesson series, search and rescue operation simulation course will be held in a resort
7 Conclusion Of all Series Lessons

- A lesson series that summarizes and conclude all lessons from the very beginning

- All simulation courses will be practiced again and new worksheets, collaboration and individual assessments will be done.
8 Mock Exam & Real On-Field Training

- A long mock exam will be held to all participants covering the very beginning of the lesson series up until the last lesson

- On-field training with different emergency courses will be done

- If there will be a good spot,real rescue operation and medical response activity will be done applying all the ways of first aid response
9 Qualifying Trainings

*FOR SECONDARY PARTICIPANTS ONLY

- Qualifying on the spot training for participants before evaluation will be give

- Evaluation of performances will be held.
